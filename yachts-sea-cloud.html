SEA CLOUD

An affluent history permeates throughout this special fleet beginning with its leader, the original SEA CLOUD. Her story starts in the upper echelon of 1931 society, when Marjorie Merriweather Post and her husband, Edward F. Hutton, commissioned a grand four-masted barque. It would be the largest private sailing yacht in the world at the time, and served as a sophisticated stage for their extravagant life hosting nobility, dignitaries, and celebrities.

Throughout the next nine decades, SEA CLOUD, would live many lives under various names, until 1979 when she was rescued and meticulously restored. Today, she radiates the glamour of Merriweather Post's impeccable tastes for an intimate handful of guests. With original suites and classic sailing decks, she leads the fleet as one of only a few passenger ships hand-rigged without electronic controls.

details

The SEA CLOUD's opulent charm shows in the Italian marble fireplaces, French antiques, oak-paneled dining room, gleaming brass, and parquet wood. Eight of the original suites have been carefully restored to their original glamour, including Merriweather's, Hutton's, and their daughter Dina Merrill's. The rest pamper guests with modern amenities in a contemporary ambiance of light and warm woods.

Even the bath fixtures exemplify the splendor on board with golden swans in the suites. The SEA CLOUD offers an intimate sailing experience with only 32 suites and staterooms, creating an ambiance with the feel of a private yacht. Guests quickly find a favorite space, from the lido bar for alfresco lunch and sunset cocktails and the open-air blue lagoon to the leisure lounge for expert lectures.

The SEA CLOUD combines opulent charm with the thrill of an authentic windjammer — even the masts are made of wood rather than aluminum. Italian marble fireplaces, French antiques, an oak-paneled dining room, golden swan bath fixtures, gleaming brass, and parquet wood exemplify the splendor aboard. Guests quickly find a favorite space, from the lido bar for alfresco lunch and sunset cocktails and the open-air blue lagoon to the leisure lounge for expert lectures, the common areas add to its historic allure.
