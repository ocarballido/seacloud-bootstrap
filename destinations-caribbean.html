The Lesser Antilles

Dive the shipwrecks, stand under waterfalls, shop the boutiques, or relax and enjoy the rhythm of island life. The Caribbean is calling with luxury sailings to her most upscale islands including St. Barths and Îles des Saintes, plus overnights in distinctions destinations mainstream cruise liners are too large to visit.

USA, Bahamas, Puerto Rico

Sail out of Miami with a champagne toast on your way to exclusive Bahamian islands that call to A-listers looking for privacy. Away from the hustle and bustle of the mainstream, we call on hidden gems in this island chain that entice travelers with untouched beaches, clear shallow waters, and extraordinary coral gardens. Divers, snorkelers, and beachcombers find their favorite spots among the seclusion, which can only be reached by yacht or tender.

Charting to the southeast, locals call Puerto Rico “la isla del encanto,” and the capital of San Juan is its heart and soul. In Old Town, colorful buildings with flowering balconies line blue cobblestoned streets, and famous historic forts—El Morro, San Cristóbal, and La Fortaleza—stand guard over the harbor. More than 500 years old, San Juan is the Puerto Rico’s most populous city with 340,000+ residents and most important port. And don’t miss a trip to new San Juan's Condado district, which sparkles with modern skyscrapers, luxury shopping, and world-class dining.

The LesseR
Antilles

From the Panama Canal and mystical Mayan ruins to the pure relaxation of the Pacific Coast, embrace the “pura vida” of life on a luxury sailing vacation. In Panama, there’s no better way to pass through the grand locks than aboard an authentic tall ship. Sail to secluded islands, where the remote access adds to the charm, and then on to the contrast vibrant cosmopolitan capitals. Snorkel both the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans before charting north to Costa Rica’s nature reserves and national parks. And on some sailings, renowned ecotourism experts join as guests hosts to make sure you don’t miss a thing.
